A master meter measures total building consumption from the utility, while submeters measure consumption for individual spaces or systems within the building. Understanding this hierarchy is essential for accurate landlord/tenant splits.

<Note>
**How Abisko Expands from ESPM:** ESPM treats all meters equally without hierarchy relationships. Abisko tracks master-submeter relationships so you can verify submeters sum correctly and identify missing consumption.
</Note>

## Meter Hierarchy

```
Master Meter (Utility Account)
├── Submeter: Common Areas
├── Submeter: Tenant A
├── Submeter: Tenant B
└── Submeter: Tenant C
```

## Key Concepts

| Meter Type | Measures | Typical Use |
|------------|----------|-------------|
| **Master meter** | Total building from utility | Whole building reporting |
| **Submeter** | Individual space/tenant | Landlord/tenant allocation |
| **Check meter** | Verification of another meter | Billing accuracy |

<Tip>
**Validation Rule:** Sum of submeters should approximately equal master meter. Significant differences indicate missing consumption or meter issues.
</Tip>

## Common Configurations

**Configuration A: Full Submetering**
- Master meter at utility entrance
- Every tenant has dedicated submeter
- Base building has separate meter(s)
- *Abisko can fully allocate landlord vs tenant*

**Configuration B: Partial Submetering**
- Master meter only
- Some tenants submetered, others estimated
- *Abisko calculates base building as: Master - Σ(submeters)*

**Configuration C: No Submetering**
- Master meter only
- Allocation by square footage or lease terms
- *Less accurate but GRESB-acceptable with documentation*

## Viewing in Abisko

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open meter list">
    **Property** → **Meters** tab
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Check meter type column">
    "Type" indicates Master, Submeter, or Check meter
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Review hierarchy">
    Click master meter to see linked submeters
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Run validation">
    **Data Quality** → Check for master/submeter discrepancies
  </Step>
</Steps>
