Gaps are periods where expected meter readings are missing. They reduce data completeness and may require gap-filling before GRESB submission.

## Types of Gaps

| Gap Type | Cause | Resolution |
|----------|-------|------------|
| **Billing gap** | Utility skipped a read | Request historical data |
| **Sync gap** | ESPM import failed | Re-sync or manual entry |
| **Meter gap** | Meter offline/replaced | Document or estimate |
| **Ownership gap** | Property acquired mid-period | Adjust ownership dates |

## Identifying Gaps in Abisko

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open Data Quality">
    **Data Quality** → **Gaps** tab
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Review flagged meters">
    List shows all meters with missing periods
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Click to see details">
    View exact date ranges missing
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Choose resolution">
    Collect data, estimate, or document why unavailable
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Gap Resolution Options

| Option | When to Use | GRESB Acceptance |
|--------|-------------|------------------|
| **Collect actual data** | Data exists but wasn't imported | ✅ Preferred |
| **Gap-fill estimation** | Data truly unavailable | ✅ With methodology |
| **Exclude property** | Major gaps, unreliable data | ⚠️ Last resort |

<Tip>
**Best Practice:** Address gaps early in the year when you have time to collect actual data from utilities or tenants.
</Tip>
