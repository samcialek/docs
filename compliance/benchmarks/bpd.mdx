The Building Performance Database (BPD) is a free tool from the U.S. Department of Energy that provides building performance data for peer comparison. It aggregates data from multiple sources for statistical analysis.

## What BPD Offers

| Feature | Description |
|---------|-------------|
| **Peer comparison** | Compare your building to similar properties |
| **Statistical analysis** | Distribution of EUI by building type |
| **Geographic filtering** | Regional and climate zone analysis |
| **Anonymous data** | Aggregated without identifying buildings |

## BPD vs ENERGY STAR

| Aspect | BPD | ENERGY STAR |
|--------|-----|-------------|
| **Purpose** | Statistical analysis | Certification/marketing |
| **Data** | Multiple sources | ESPM submissions only |
| **Filters** | Highly customizable | Fixed adjustments |
| **Output** | Statistical comparison | Single score |

## When to Use BPD

- **Due diligence:** Evaluating acquisition targets
- **Portfolio positioning:** Understanding where buildings rank
- **Research:** Analyzing market segments
- **Custom benchmarking:** When ENERGY STAR doesn't cover your building type

<Tip>
**Coverage:** BPD includes building types not eligible for ENERGY STAR scores, making it useful for specialty properties.
</Tip>
